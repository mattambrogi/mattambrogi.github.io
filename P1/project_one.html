Donations Platform

Designing a better way to give

Overview

This fall, I came across a peice of research that concluded that American's donate far less to charity than they feel they actually should. The numbers said that while Americans felt that they should donate around 6% of their salary, the actual number fell closer to 3%. This gap is equivalent to a $250B loss in potential donations. I immediately wondered, what could help close this gap?

The Problem

The problem I observed was that donations are a haphazard business. They tend to be done sporadically with a fair deal of randomness, based on marketing, in who the recipient organization is. I began to imagine a central hub for donations. If it was as easy to keep track of and make donations to charitable organizations as something like Robinhood has made it invest in public corporations, maybe more people would do it more frequently.
